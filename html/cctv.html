CCTV Policies

General Guidelines

  • Implementation of professional, ethical and legal manner of video monitoring in PHI Building for security purpose. It prohibits in monitoring of individuals based on characteristics of race, gender, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected classifications.
  • The Building Admin Office can monitor the recorded images captured by the system, twenty-four (24) hours a day and seven (7) days a week. CCTV system installed shall be capable to record and store for at least (30) thirty days.
  • Access for the CCTV system shall be only limited to the following persons:
    • Building Administrator/ Enginee
    • Admin Assistant
    • Authorized Building Technician or Security Personnel
    • Department Manager or Supervisor
    • Legal, MIS and EHSS Officers
    • CCTV technician (supplier)
  • CCTV recordings shall only be disclosed to law officers and the extent of the recordings handed to them must pertain only to the following instances:
    • In an investigation of an offense punishable by law---the manner in which the offense was committed and/or to ascertain the identity of the perpetrator; or
    • Used as evidence to criminal, civil or administrative proceedings, subject to court order.
  • Information received and acquired by the Building Administrator, his/her staff or security personnel, from the police officer or from the complainant regarding the details of the investigation and other information relevant thereto, shall be considered as confidential in nature and the same shall not be divulged to any person except those allowed by the company and this policy.

Procedures for request to copy or view CCTV recordings

Request by Police Officers

  • The security guard assigned at the main entrance shall ask for proper identification, before entry of police officers and after stating their purpose of the conduct of investigation, in case, they are in civilian attire.
  • The security guard shall immediately relay the information to the Building Administrator and shall accompany the police officers to the Administrator’s Office, as the case maybe.
  • The Building Administrator shall ask for a written request from the most senior police officer who desires to view and/or copy any footage, in aid of investigation of an incident. The written request must contain the following information:
    • Name of the police unit and address
    • Name of the requesting party
    • Contact number of the requesting party
    • Date of request
    • Purpose of the viewing and/or copying
    • Name of the subject
    • Approximate dates and time when the subject was spotted
    • Complete address and exact location where the subject was spotted
    • Name of the complainant
  • The Building Administrator shall keep the written request and submit a copy to Safety Officer for future reference. Moreover, consider the request as highly confidential and any contents not be disclosed to anybody except those persons authorized in this policy.
  • The Building Administrator, if necessary, may bring in or invite the police officers to his office in order to maintain the confidentiality of the information and to limit the presence of police officers in the lobby that may cause anxiety, distraction or alarm to the occupants of the building.
  • In the absence of the authorized technician, the Building Engineer shall review and copy the desired footage based on the details indicated on the written request. At the same time, retain a copy of the subject CCTV footage, which must be stored in an appropriate file for company’s use and reference in the future.
  • The Building Administrator shall accomplish a report, which contains details and circumstances surrounding the event of investigation conducted by the police officers, together with pertinent documents and shall submit the same to his immediate superior and EHSS for their information.

Request by the Complainant

  • Upon receipt of complaint, the Building Administrator or his staff shall require the complainant to fill out the CCTV request form which contains the following details:
    • Date of request
    • Name of tenant/lessee/unit owner
    • Unit number or company
    • Contact number
    • Cause of the complaint
    • Description of the properties lost, if any
    • Date and the approximate time of the incident
    • Time approved
    • Other relevant details
  • The CCTV request shall be approved by the Building Administrator’s Supervisor or Manager.
  • After approval, the Building Engineer or the authorized technician shall review the footages based on the details provided in the CCTV Request Form.
  • The Building Engineer after having reviewed the footages shall submit to the Building Administrator a written report within 24 hours from the approval of the request form, which shall indicate, among others, his findings on the review of the CCTV recordings. He shall also retain a copy of the subject CCTV footage, which must be stored in an appropriate file for company’s use and reference in the future.
  • The Building Administrator shall inform the complainant who requested the CCTV footage, within a reasonable time, as to the result of the CCTV footage review based on the written report submitted to him. If the complainant wishes to obtain a copy of the report, prior to the release of report the Building Administrator shall first seek approval from his direct superior.
  • In case the complainant demands to copy or view the CCTV footages relating to an incident or offense, the Building Administrator shall advise the former to secure a Police Blotter Report or seek assistance from the police authorities for the investigation of the incident. If the latter case shall happen, the Building Administrator shall observe the procedures on “Request by Police Officers” cited in the preceding title.
  • The Building Administrator shall submit the request form together with the CCTV report to EHSS for information and reference.

By Court Order

  • Any person who desires to obtain a copy of the CCTV footages, used as evidence, in relation to an offense already filed in court, shall secure an order for the release of the said footages from the court or any other tribunal, which has jurisdiction or agencies authorized by law to issue subpoena.
